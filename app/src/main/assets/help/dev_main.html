Saving a game

When you're entering Town & Danger levels, if a location doesn't have a number token, you don't need to enter the number printed on the map; just enter the tokens which are on the board.

Saving draw & discard states

If you're only playing one campaign at a time, then you don't need to worry about this. (That is, if you can arrange the cards so that your face-down draw piles are stacked on top of their face-up discard piles, and have them still be that way the next time you play so that you can just pull them out of the box & put them back the way they were, then you don't need the app to record which cards were in your discard piles.)

However, if you're running more than one campaign at a time—playing with one group on Friday night, and another group on Saturday night—and you want to preserve your discard piles, you can do that with a little extra work.

First, turn on "Save discard piles" in Settings. This will show you the list of decks at the bottom of the campaign record sheet, and you can click on them to bring up the list of cards in the deck.

Next, have another player run through the cards in the discards, reading off their names, while you find them in the app's alphabetically sorted list and mark them as discarded.

Editing a saved game

You probably never actually want to do this. The exception is if you realize you forgot to record a token or whatever; in that case, use the Edit button to go back and update the saved campaign. But, in general, you probably want to choose Save, pick the point in the campaign you want to copy from, and go from there.
